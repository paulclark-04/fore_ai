# Persona Patterns — New Persona Types Discovered Through Feedback

## CDO / Chief Data Officer Pattern
- **Examples**: Helmi BOUHLEL (CDO at Veepee, score 83), Quentin Goffinon (Deputy CDO at Boulanger, A-tier)
- **Pattern**: CDOs have budget authority and transformation mandates. Data quality = software quality adjacency.
- **Rule**: Do NOT under-score CDOs. They are A-tier when at target companies with digital/data transformation scope.
- **Typical score range**: 80-88

## Innovation Scout with Budget Pattern (Nicolas Sorre Pattern)
- **Example**: Nicolas Sorre — Head of Smart Factory at Airbus, score 81
- **Pattern**: Innovation leader with explicit budget (euros mentioned) + startup mandate + software/IT DNA in past roles
- **Rule**: Budget + software DNA can override manufacturing/vertical mismatch. Exceptional sponsor → A tier.
- **Key signals**: Budget amounts, startup scouting, POC mandate, past software/cloud/AI roles

## Engineering Manager with QA Awareness (Flavien Pattern)
- **Example**: Flavien Moutawe — Engineering Manager, EngineeringOps at Veepee, score 78
- **Pattern**: Engineering manager who uses QA tools (Playwright, Sonar) and advocates quality culture, but is a delivery manager not QA owner
- **Rule**: Cap at 78. Strong B-tier, NOT A-tier. Delivery manager ≠ QA owner.

## VP Technical Product Owner (Jeremy Pattern)
- **Example**: Jeremy Signoret — VP Technical PO/PM at Lombard Odier, score 80
- **Pattern**: VP-level product owner delivering web applications with explicit tech stack (J2EE, Spring)
- **Rule**: VP + PO combo checked BEFORE C-Level detection. Score A-tier (80-85) when in banking/finance with legacy migration pain.

## Massive IT Scope Sponsor → A-tier (Arnaud Raffray Pattern)
- **Example**: Arnaud Raffray — Head of IM, 350 people, 500+ apps at Airbus, score 80
- **Pattern**: Sponsor role but with enterprise-scale IT scope (hundreds of people, hundreds of apps)
- **Rule**: Categories describe relationship type, not tier ceiling. Exceptional sponsor with massive scope = A-tier.

## AI/GenAI Integration Lead as Champion (Frederic VOIRIN Pattern)
- **Example**: Frederic VOIRIN — AI Program Manager at CA PFM, score 83
- **Pattern**: Person whose mandate is to integrate AI across the enterprise — evaluating, piloting, and industrializing AI solutions company-wide. They are the natural internal champion for AI-powered tools like Fore AI.
- **Rule**: A-tier (80-85) as Sponsor/Champion. Their job is literally to find and deploy AI tools. Fore AI's autonomous QA agents fit directly into their "agentic AI" evaluation pipeline. Don't confuse with "AI Researcher" or "ML Engineer" who build models — the integration/program manager is the one who BUYS and DEPLOYS AI tools.
- **Key signals**: "intégrer l'IA", "industrialiser", "agentic", "plateforme IA", "AI program", "transformation IA"
- **Discovered**: CA Personal Finance & Mobility batch

## IT Cost Optimization Buyer (Jean-Hugues Seret Pattern)
- **Example**: Jean-Hugues Seret — "IT Cost Cutting Plan and Purchasing Manager" at CA Consumer Finance, score 84
- **Pattern**: IT leader with an explicit cost-reduction mandate AND purchasing authority. Fore AI's core pitch IS cost optimization (10x faster, 60-80% cheaper than manual QA). This person's KPIs align directly with what Fore AI delivers.
- **Rule**: A-tier (80-85) as Primary Buyer. This is NOT a Procurement Gate — this is someone actively looking for tools that cut IT costs. They have both the mandate (find savings) and the mechanism (purchasing power) to buy.
- **Key signals**: "cost cutting", "optimisation des coûts IT", "purchasing manager IT", "réduction budgétaire", "achats IT", budget authority + cost mandate
- **Distinction from Procurement Gate**: A Procurement Gate (C-tier) processes purchase orders but doesn't initiate them. An IT Cost Optimization Buyer (A-tier) actively seeks tools that reduce costs — they initiate AND approve.
- **Discovered**: CA Personal Finance & Mobility batch

## Product Owner with Dev Squad in Banking (Jules Viron Corrected Pattern)
- **Example**: Jules Viron — "PO Squad Tunnels de transformation" at CA PFM, managing developers + analysts in scrum
- **Pattern**: PO running an agile squad with actual developers, working on web transformation. Despite a misleading "marketing digital" headline, the current role involves technical delivery of web application improvements.
- **Rule**: B-tier (72-78) as Sponsor. Has dev team, feels QA pain, but "tunnels de transformation" can be UX/conversion-focused rather than pure engineering. Check job description for software delivery keywords (recette, backlog technique, mise en production) to distinguish from conversion-focused POs.
- **Key lesson**: Always check enrichment data / current role description, not just the headline.
- **Discovered**: CA Personal Finance & Mobility batch
